# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

CCL is a tool for developers. We want it to be built by a community that is
welcoming to everyone — regardless of experience level, background, or
how they use Claude Code.

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience
for everyone.

## Standards

**Behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Being direct, constructive, and kind
- Accepting feedback without defensiveness — and giving it without cruelty
- Disagreeing about technical decisions without making it personal
- Helping newcomers get oriented without condescension
- Acknowledging when you are wrong, and moving forward

**Behaviour that is not acceptable:**

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks of any kind
- Dismissing contributors because of their experience level
- Trolling, deliberately derailing threads, or bad-faith engagement
- Publishing someone's private information without consent
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate
  in a professional setting

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull
requests, discussions, and any other venue where CCL is being worked on.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour can be reported by opening a GitHub
issue with the label `conduct`, or by emailing the maintainers directly
(address in `package.json`).

All reports will be reviewed and investigated. Maintainers are obligated to
maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter.

Consequences for violations are at the discretion of the maintainers and
may include a warning, temporary ban, or permanent removal from the project.
